Parkinson's Disease

What factors lead to greater risk,
An exploratory analysis

Following a similar structure to Hans Rosling's gapminder, we visualise the prevalence of Parkinson's Disease. We compare this prevalence against the percentage of new cases of parkinsons (Incidence) in each country and the corresponding sugar consumption. The data is also categorised by region and gender in hopes that we may see a trend over time within these categories. Data Source

Brief Guide

Prevalence:

Referring to the proportion of people in a population who are a case of Parkinson's Disease

Incidence:

Number of new cases of Parkinson's Disease each year as a percentage of the population

Y-Axis:

Incidence of Parkinson's Disease for that year as a percentage of population

X-Axis:

All risk variables are measured in Years lived with disability (YLDs) as a percentage of the population. The x-axis scale can also be presented as a log scale. Definition of variables below can be found here.

Years lived with
disability (YLDs)		 Years lived with any short-term or long-term health loss weighted for severity by the disability weights
Risk (Risk factor) An attribute, behavior, exposure, or other factor which is causally associated with an increased (or decreased)
probability of a disease or injury. If the probability decreased, the risk is a protective factor.
